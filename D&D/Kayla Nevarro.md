She is talkative, bubbly and warm. She is bored by how people only seem to want to gamble here in her hometown of Las Vegas, and how everyone seems to want to cater to their needs. Therefore, when meeting people that break that mould, Kayla will quickly befriend them. 

In some ways, she does genuinely love Las Vegas. The grandiose spectacles, the performances, the enormity of it all. She just dislikes that, in the end, it all revolves about making money.

Kayla grew up backstage. Her mom (Lucia) was trying her hardest to make a living as an entertainer in the Circus Circus. Then the hotel changed direction. Lucia had to be let go. Live performers were gradually cut back in favor of cheaper automated attractions and gaming revenue. Lucia’s contract ended along with many others.

Instead, her mother took on a job there as part of the staff. For Kayla, that transition felt humiliating. Her mom had gone from being part of the show to cleaning up after it. She still dislikes the place for that reason, but often finds herself there anyways helping out her mom when needed. Through helping, she has become quite familiar with the Circus Circus and its dingy maintenance routes. She knows most of the staff, if not by name, then by face. 

She has also seen how the Circus Circus operates in the quiet hours, when there are no tourists around. It's almost a completely different place when it is deserted like that, and Kayla usually gets creeped out.
